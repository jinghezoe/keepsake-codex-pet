# Consent and care

## Living person

Confirm permission for both creation and intended audience. Private creation permission is not automatically public-posting permission. Let the subject reject inaccurate or uncomfortable details.

## Memorial use

Keep the first version private. Consult close family or relevant rights holders before public release. Preserve observable memories rather than inventing new speech, beliefs, or messages. Never frame the pet as replacing the person.

## Source handling

- use only the minimum approved references;
- avoid unrelated people, private chats, documents, addresses, and location clues;
- do not copy source photos into the installed pet package;
- do not publish raw references unless the affected people explicitly approve that audience;
- remove metadata before sharing visual files;
- keep tokens, account data, and model credentials out of every run and repository.

## Sensitive content

Do not infer protected or sensitive traits from appearance. Do not sexualize, humiliate, or create degrading failure animations. For children, require guardian permission and minimize publication.
